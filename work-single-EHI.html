EHI!

Ehi! Is a nightlife event-finding mobile application that allows users to meet new people through shared interests.



Role Concepet development,
Visual Design, Prototyping
Teammates Michela Burzio
Nydia Chavarria
Andrew Fair
Course Designing Mobile Interfaces Supervisors Spark Replay team

This application is targeted at newcomers and bold locals, currently living in Lugano, Switzerland, who are looking to enhance their nightlife in the city and willing to meet new friends.

This application is seeking to overcome communication and cultural barriers that currently affect the social nightlife of our future users.

Interface
This application was designed with the objective to communicate that its users are young and adventurous people, who love to have a social nightlife and who are also looking for excitement and fun.

Upon login or registration, Ehi! first resembles a typical online event or concert-finding board, where events are listed from top to bottom based on relevance, date, etc. This mentioned similarity is likely to fit users’ existing cognitive models and make the app quite easy to learn.


User Experience
The development of application involved user interviews and user testing. The concept rose out of the anecdotal experiences of our group members and interviewees. Many newcomers that live in lugano find it difficult to make new friends and to find nightlife events to attend. And for the locals in particular, they showed also interest in meeting new people and having an additional platform to be informed about nightlife events in the city.


During the user testing, we realized the value of making the user’s journey feel natural. Hence the importance of using correctly the swiping and tapping modalities. The user experience is also complemented by the signature screen and social interaction that the application provides after the attendance to the event. With this application, the user has the opportunity to attend to events of his or her interest and also meet a new group of friends to complement the experience.
